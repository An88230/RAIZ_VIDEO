# Content Safety and Rights

Content Radar is an analysis layer. It is not a copying layer.

## Hard Rules

- Do not download videos.
- Do not repost videos.
- Do not remove watermarks.
- Do not bypass copyright.
- Do not clone creator identity, voice, logo, or exact script.
- Do not implement scraping in this phase.
- Do not upload or publish.
- Do not call YouTube, Google Drive, or n8n.
- Do not execute arbitrary shell commands.

## Pattern-Only Analysis

Allowed:

- hook category
- pacing pattern
- problem/payoff structure
- product category
- broad visual sequence
- audience pain
- production difficulty
- affiliate opportunity
- safety risks
- original remake idea

Not allowed:

- source footage reuse
- source audio reuse
- exact captions or script
- watermark removal
- logo or brand misuse
- copying a creator's distinctive identity
- claiming ownership of someone else's footage

## Source Rights Model

Every pattern report must state:

- `pattern_only: true`
- `copies_source_video: false`
- `uses_source_audio: false`
- `watermark_removal_required: false`
- `requires_original_assets: true`

If those cannot be true, the opportunity must be rejected or rewritten.

## Affiliate Safety

Affiliate angles must be truthful. They should describe a problem and a product
category without claiming results that are not verified.

Required:

- disclose commercial intent in future publishing workflows
- avoid medical, financial, legal, or hazardous claims without review
- prefer product categories over brand claims unless a licensed relationship
  exists
- avoid fake urgency and fake scarcity

## Home and Maintenance Safety

Home repair, cleaning, and appliance maintenance ideas need a safety review.
Flag:

- electrical work
- plumbing work
- chemical mixing
- ladder use
- sharp tools
- fire, heat, or pressure risks
- appliance disassembly

When risk is high, the remake idea should become a safe checklist, warning, or
professional-help recommendation instead of an instructional demo.

## Useful Curiosity Filter

RAIZ should prefer useful, surprising, practical, or problem-solving content.
Gross-out, disgust-first, injury-first, humiliation-first, and shock-only
formats are excluded from default radar behavior.
